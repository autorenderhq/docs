## Overview

AutoRender's zoom feature allows you to magnify or reduce specific regions of an image before applying other transformations. Zoom is particularly useful for focusing on details, creating close-up views, or adjusting the effective crop area of an image.

### URL Structure

AutoRender URLs with zoom follow this structure:


## How Zoom Works

Zoom works by cropping the image to a smaller region and then resizing it back to the original dimensions (or your specified dimensions). This creates the effect of "zooming in" on a specific area.

- **Zoom factor > 1.0**: Zooms in (crops to a smaller area, then enlarges)
- **Zoom factor = 1.0**: No zoom (original image)
- **Zoom factor < 1.0**: Zooms out (shows more of the image)

### Available Positions

You can use any position parameter with zoom:

- `p_c` - Center (default)
- `p_tl` - Top-left
- `p_tr` - Top-right
- `p_bl` - Bottom-left
- `p_br` - Bottom-right
- `p_t` - Top center
- `p_b` - Bottom center
- `p_l` - Left center
- `p_r` - Right center
- `p_face` - Face detection (if available)
- `p_entropy` - Entropy-based positioning
- `p_attention` - Attention-based positioning

## Best Practices

### Zoom Factor Guidelines

- **1.0 - 1.3**: Subtle zoom, minimal quality loss
- **1.3 - 2.0**: Moderate zoom, acceptable for most use cases
- **2.0 - 3.0**: Strong zoom, may show quality degradation
- **> 3.0**: Extreme zoom, significant quality loss likely

### Quality Considerations

- Higher zoom factors reduce effective resolution
- Combine with high-quality source images when possible
- Use appropriate output formats (WebP, AVIF) to maintain quality
- Consider the final display size when choosing zoom factor

### Performance

- Zoom processing is fast and efficient
- Zoom is applied before resize operations
- Cached results improve performance for repeated requests
