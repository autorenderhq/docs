## Overview

The contrast effect adjusts the difference between light and dark areas in your images. Higher contrast makes images more dramatic, while lower contrast creates a softer, more muted look.

### URL Structure

AutoRender URLs with contrast follow this structure:

```bash
https://assets.autorender.io/LOKVTtKVGb/e_contrast:30/docs/example/porsche.jpg
```

## Contrast (`e_contrast`)

Adjusts the difference between light and dark areas.

**Format:** `e_contrast:{level}`
- **Range:** `-100` to `100`
- **Negative values:** Reduce contrast (softer)
- **Positive values:** Increase contrast (more dramatic)

```bash
https://assets.autorender.io/LOKVTtKVGb/e_contrast:30/docs/example/porsche.jpg
```

## Use Cases

- **Dramatic effects:** Increase contrast for bold, striking images
- **Soft portraits:** Reduce contrast for gentle, flattering portraits
- **Product photography:** Enhance contrast to make products pop
- **Landscape photography:** Adjust contrast to emphasize details

## Combining with Other Effects

Contrast works well with brightness and saturation:

```bash
https://assets.autorender.io/LOKVTtKVGb/e_contrast:25,e_brightness:10,e_saturation:15/docs/example/porsche.jpg
```

<img src="https://assets.autorender.io/LOKVTtKVGb/e_contrast:25,e_brightness:10,e_saturation:15/docs/example/porsche.jpg" alt="Contrast with brightness and saturation" class="ar-url-image" />

## Best Practices

- Moderate contrast (20-40) usually looks most natural
- High contrast can make images look harsh
- Low contrast creates a dreamy, soft effect
- Combine with brightness for optimal results
